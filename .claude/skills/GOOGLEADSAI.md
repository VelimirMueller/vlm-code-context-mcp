Google Ads AI-Powered Marketing – Skills & Best Practices

A comprehensive reference guide covering Google's AI-driven advertising solutions, Smart Bidding, Performance Max, campaign experiments, and optimization best practices.


1. BUDGETS & FLEXIBILITY
   Q: What best practice should a digital marketing manager follow to maximize results from a fully flexible budget?
   A: Use shared budgets and portfolio bid strategies to maximize campaign flexibility and allow AI to do its work.
   Q: Why is it important to uncap budgets to fully capture the benefits of Google's AI?
   A: Campaigns need sufficient budget headroom to allow Google's AI to work to its fullest potential.
   Key Principle: A flexible budget only creates value if actively managed — shift spend toward top-performing channels in real time using performance data.

2. SMART BIDDING STRATEGIES
   Q: Which Smart Bidding strategy optimizes for value?
   A: Target ROAS (Return on Ad Spend).
   Q: A digital marketing manager wants to drive more value but needs to operate within a fixed budget. Which Smart Bidding strategy is the right fit?
   A: Target ROAS.
   Q: An online retailer needs to achieve a target return metric set by their CMO. Which Smart Bidding strategy is the right fit?
   A: Target ROAS.
   Q: A marketer has upgraded to value-based bidding. Which two Google Ads tools should they use regularly?
   A: Performance Planner and Bid Strategy Report.
   Q: Why would value-based bidding be the right solution for lead generation campaigns?
   A: Value-based bidding uses AI to maximize total conversion value at a desired ROAS target or budget.

3. VALUE-BASED BIDDING
   Q: What's a best practice for a business starting value-based bidding for the first time?
   A: Begin with a strong measurement and attribution strategy based on their goals.
   Q: What's an example of a metric used to measure value?
   A: Cart size.
   Q: How can advertisers with a lead generation objective optimize Performance Max campaigns for value?
   A: Create a strategy of importing offline values by using enhanced conversions for leads.
   Key Principles:

Assign different conversion values to different customer actions to reflect their true business worth.
Use enhanced conversions to import offline data and close the measurement loop.
Build value rules based on audience segments, devices, or locations to differentiate high-value customers.


4. KEYWORD MATCH TYPES
   Q: Which match type is the default match type to which all keywords are assigned?
   A: Broad match.
   Q: What's a benefit of using broad match keywords?
   A: Broad match allows marketers to reach the most possible consumers searching for their product or service, while saving time from adding a variety of keywords.
   Q: What's a benefit of negative keywords?
   A: Negative keywords prevent an otherwise eligible query from triggering an ad that isn't aligned with the search's intent.
   Q: A digital marketing manager upgraded to broad match. Which behavior should they build into their regular optimization cadence?
   A: Remove negative keywords that may block relevant traffic.

   A: Build capabilities in key areas such as high-quality data and a skilled workforce.

6. PERFORMANCE MAX CAMPAIGNS
   Q: What goal should a local automobile dealership choose when setting up a Performance Max campaign to drive more location visits?
   A: Local store visits and promotions.
   Q: What's a best practice when designing an experiment to test Performance Max campaigns?
   A: Optimize Performance Max to a comparable CPA or ROAS target to other campaigns.
   Key Best Practices for Performance Max:

Provide a full asset group: up to 15 headlines, 5 descriptions, multiple images, logos, and videos.
Link Google Merchant Center or Google My Business feeds where applicable.
Set clear conversion goals before launch.
Allow a learning period of at least 4–6 weeks before evaluating results.
Use audience signals to guide (not restrict) Google's AI.


7. RESPONSIVE SEARCH ADS (RSA)
   Q: What action can a digital marketing manager take to improve Ad Strength for their RSAs?
   A: They can create more unique headlines and descriptions.
   Key Best Practices for RSAs:

Use all 15 headline slots and all 4 description slots.
Avoid pinning assets unless absolutely necessary — pinning limits AI optimization.
Include top keywords in at least 3–5 headlines.
Use the Ad Strength indicator (Poor → Excellent) as a guide.
Leverage the Combinations Report to see which asset combinations perform best.


8. ASSET REPORTS & CREATIVE OPTIMIZATION
   Q: What are two ways the Combination and Assets Report can help improve creative asset performance?
   A:

Identify opportunities to replace low-performing assets with assets that have better potential.
Use high-performing assets as inspiration for future creative assets.

Key Principle: Regularly audit asset performance ratings (Low / Good / Best) and replace "Low" rated assets with fresh creative inspired by "Best" rated ones.

10. OPTIMIZATION SCORE & RECOMMENDATIONS
    Q: What's an important benefit of opting into automatically applied recommendations?
    A: AI decides which recommendations to apply automatically to campaigns, without any need for human input.
    Q: What factors contribute to an optimization score recommendation being surfaced?
    A: Insights gained from machine learning and simulations that identify potential performance uplift based on a marketer's business objective.
    Key Principles:

Optimization Score ranges from 0%–100%; 100% means the account is fully optimized.
Recommendations are tailored to account history, campaign settings, and Google trends.
Auto-apply is best used for low-risk recommendations (e.g., adding sitelinks, upgrading match types).
Always review recommendations in context of your specific business goals before applying.


11. CAMPAIGN EXPERIMENTS
    Q: What's the value of using campaign experiments to test campaign optimizations?
    A: Campaign experiments can help marketers understand whether proposed changes help reach their desired marketing objective.
    Q: Which is a best practice when creating a campaign experiment?
    A: Focus tests on one variable at a time and use separate tests to examine the effects of more than one change.
    Q: What's a best practice when designing an experiment to test Performance Max?
    A: Optimize Performance Max to a comparable CPA or ROAS target to other campaigns.
    Experimentation Best Practices:

Test one variable at a time (bidding strategy, match type, creative, landing page).
Run experiments long enough to achieve statistical significance (typically 4+ weeks).
Evaluate results after the ramp-up/learning period has ended.
Use a meaningful traffic split (e.g., 50/50) for clear comparisons.
Define success metrics before launching — don't change KPIs mid-test.


12. MEASUREMENT & CONVERSION TRACKING
    Q: What's the first best practice a business should follow when starting value-based bidding?
    A: Begin with a strong measurement and attribution strategy based on their goals.
    Key Measurement Principles:

Set up conversion tracking before launching campaigns — never after.
Use data-driven attribution (DDA) to give proper credit across touchpoints.
Import offline conversions (CRM data, store visits) to give AI full signal.
Use enhanced conversions to improve measurement accuracy using first-party data.
Align conversion actions to actual business outcomes (revenue, leads, store visits).


13. AUDIENCE & DATA STRATEGY
    Q: Why is first-party data valuable to fueling Google's AI?
    A: It's high-quality and captures customer transactions that drive business results.
    Key Principles:

First-party data (CRM lists, site visitors, purchasers) is a competitive differentiator.
Use Customer Match to upload customer lists and reach known audiences.
Segment audiences by value tier (e.g., high-LTV customers vs. one-time buyers).
Build value rules to bid higher for audiences most likely to convert at high value.


14. DIGITAL LEADERSHIP & AI READINESS
    Q: What efforts should digital leaders prioritize to support Google's AI?
    A: Build capabilities in key areas such as high-quality data and a skilled workforce.
    Organizational Best Practices:

Break down data silos — AI needs unified, high-quality signals across channels.
Invest in a skilled workforce that understands AI tools and can interpret data.
Foster a culture of experimentation — testing and learning is central to AI-driven marketing.
Align marketing budgets to performance outcomes, not just activity metrics.
Treat digital marketing as a revenue driver, not a cost center.
